# Design research: outsourced help desk services

Research date: July 20, 2026

Search intent: `outsourced help desk services` and `outsourced IT help desk services company`

## Five direct competitors reviewed

### 1. 31West
URL: https://www.31west.net/services/technical-support/

A traditional lead-gen page with a corporate blue palette, a quote form beside the service copy, and a large legal footer. It makes contact options easy to find, but the gray hero, small stock image, serif body copy, and long text blocks feel dated.

### 2. SupportYourApp
URL: https://supportyourapp.com/technical-support-outsourcing/

The strongest design in the group. Its yellow split hero, large team photo, round display type, direct headline, and early client-logo bar make the page feel warm and established. Flat service panels keep the page easy to scan. The oversized headline and competing CTA labels are the main drawbacks.

### 3. Unity Communications
URL: https://unity-connect.com/services/technical-support-outsourcing/

A broad enterprise page with role cards, industry pills, capability grids, and a deep footer. The role section makes the service concrete. Mixed type styles, too many CTA labels, and several blank or low-contrast areas weaken the finish.

### 4. Buchanan Technologies
URL: https://www.buchanan.com/services/it-help-desk-services/

A polished navy, teal, and lime enterprise system with a strong image hero and a clear consultation CTA. The dot-grid motif gives the page a recognizable IT feel. In the captured state, cookie consent covered the hero action and several later modules appeared empty or unloaded.

### 5. WOW24-7
URL: https://wow24-7.com/outsourced-technical-support

A lively purple, orange, and teal page with human cutout images, support-tier content, client logos, and detailed onboarding. Its service depth is useful, but the hero is crowded and the very long page contains broken-looking blank areas and inconsistent calls to action.

## Selected inspiration

SupportYourApp is the primary reference because it explains a technical service with a human image, bold type, one strong color field, and proof close to the hero. Those ideas fit a help desk buyer better than a generic staffing template.

## Original direction

The new homepage uses a different visual language: deep navy, warm white, mint status cues, and a coral action color. The hero is a dark support brief beside a large team image, with a vertical ticket-status rail that makes the work feel tangible. A compact operating strip replaces unverified logo or performance claims. Service lanes, a first-week launch board, and a ticket-flow panel explain scope without publishing prices.

Nothing copies a competitor's brand assets, exact wording, color palette, illustration style, or page composition. Existing service, blog, contact, thank-you, legal, robots, and sitemap routes remain intact.
